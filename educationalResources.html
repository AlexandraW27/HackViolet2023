Educational Resources

Khan Academy

Khan Academy is a non-profit educational organization created in 2008 with the goal of providing a free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere. It offers a vast library of instructional videos, practice exercises, and assessments in a variety of subjects, including mathematics, science, history, and economics. The platform is accessible to anyone with an internet connection, making it a valuable resource for students, teachers, and lifelong learners.

Open Library

Open Library is an online library initiative launched in 2006 by the Internet Archive, a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving digital cultural artifacts. Open Library aims to create a free, comprehensive digital library of all published works, including books, magazines, newspapers, and more. It offers a vast collection of books that can be read online for free, as well as access to scans of millions of books in the public domain. The goal of Open Library is to provide a single page for every book ever published, with links to other digital versions, including those available for purchase or loan. It is a valuable resource for researchers, students, and anyone looking for a wide range of information on a variety of topics.

TED-Ed

TED-Ed is an educational platform created by TED, the organization known for its popular TED Talks. It provides animated lessons and educational videos on a wide range of topics, including science, technology, engineering, math, and more. The videos are created by educators and animators and aim to explain complex concepts in an engaging and visually appealing way. Additionally, TED-Ed provides resources for teachers and students, including lesson plans and discussion questions. The goal of TED-Ed is to make learning and education accessible and inspiring for people of all ages and backgrounds.
